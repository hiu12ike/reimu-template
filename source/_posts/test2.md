### Nighttime Reads: Ichigo Marshmallow

I want to start by introducing a section that will continue to show up from now on: **Nighttime Reads**. For a while now, I've developed a habit of reading manga before bed(like I don’t spend all my time reading manga). These are usually SoL to make me laugh a little and sleep happily (Although I've also been reading the Fate/Grand Order main story during the same time, but we'll talk about that some other post). Maybe I'll go over it in a different post, but this time I want to go over one I recently finished: Ichigo marshmallow. This manga took a bit for me to like but I liked it's comedy once I understood it. The hard panel cut when it comes to beating the ever living shit out of Miu is really funny.  Always gets me. Also Matsuri and Ana are in fact angels who should be protected. The series has unfortunately been on hiatus, although the author is still continuing doujin activities and privately selling his own merch of the manga. I think the manga might get a bit formulaic past a certain point for some people, but isn’t that all CGDCT? If it’s still funny, then it’s still fine.

### D-frag

This is the standout from the binges covered imo. I had picked up D-Frag before, but dropped it due to…idk to be honest. But I picked it back up on a whim during nightime reads. The reason it’s not listed under that is because I usually keep nighttime reads in…the night, and they usually don’t cross over to morning reads. It also wasn’t planned to be a nighttime read. But god this manga is a fun time. It’s the epitome of the Japanese manzai(straight man, funny man) routine taken to the absolute extreme. Being the straight man isn’t just hard, it’s literally a power the protagonist has. Only him and those from his family can do so many *tsukkomis* (usually just him and his sister Moe). Others try, but can’t reach their level of *tsukkomi* power, or get tired due to lack of lung capacity. MC **shaved his head** to win rock paper scissors. His family’s hair is so spiky that it is not only a weapon, but its literally their sole recognizable feature, where they only realized Moe was his sister because they touched her hair and it had the same spikiness. I’ll cut this off because I think I’ll give this one it’s own post. Also aliens are real lmaoooooooooo.

### Ascendance of a Bookworm 

<InfoBox type="note">This covers part 1 to part of 3</InfoBox>


I didn’t think I’d enjoy this as much as I did. It’s surprisingly realistic in its takes and how Myne can’t avoid certain parts of fate, but there’s loopholes to everything. Therefore, she’s abusing loopholes all the time to get what she wants and I kinda enjoy it. She can show preference to her birth family in part 3, and write letters to them without breaking the magic contract. Parts 1 and 2 are pretty good. I also like how slowly paced it is. It’s literally only been like 3 years or so in-universe. The content actually feels like a year’s worth of stuff happening, and progress feels well paced. You know, for being the most amount of content on this list, I don’t have much to say about it other than it’s nice. I might watch the part 3 anime since it’ll probably get through content before the manga finishes. Too busy to read the LN.

Temp rating: 70

### Slime Diaries

I’ve been getting back into Tensura recently, albeit only the manga. However, I also binged all of Slime Diaries, the 4koma spinoff. Since it’s written by Fuze himself, most events in the story are canon, except for when it parodies a serious event and makes fun. Yuuki gets a lot more characterization in this manga, and it kinda shows his playfulness and how he treats everything like a game. I believe this is on purpose, especially because the manga itself takes some events seriously. It did a long, serious take on the Tensura massacre, which honestly hit harder than the main show, partly because of the contrast between the usual goofy nature of Slime Diaries and the serious take it did on the massacre. Also really shows how Yuuki is the only always non-serious character.  The entire manga is a fun time. Also like how it actually incorporates the Trinity in Tempest characters and plot. I haven’t read it but the manga makes it obvious they’re there.

Temp rating: 76

### Star Detective Precure

<InfoBox type="note">This covers Episode 10 and 11.</InfoBox>

Cure Arcana Shadow is here! Her popularity before her full reveal and allat has been unmatched for the franchise. All she did was eat ice cream and be the newbie in the villain team, too. But her merch keeps selling out and having to be limited. For the first time in franchise history, a character read the sponsor messages in the anime. She was THAT popular before her release. And Toei noticed. They released her transformation debut episode on Youtube without region lock even before Crunchyroll uploaded the translation. They banked all in after seeing this early popularity, and it paid off. According to some people I know, her art SPIKED way more than it already did. And the episode itself? Really good. The phantom thieves actually did phantom thief shit. And then there was Luluka’s transformation which might be one of the best in the entire franchise. It’s a wonder. Her transformations into both a police officer in episode 10 and librarian in ep11 are both pretty cute. I’m interested in hearing Luluka’s history with the Curetto Detective Agency. Also based on her information in ep11, she stalks the procure. It’s not weird, especially because the entire guild already knows that’s their home base, which is a very interesting concept and I hope they use that knowledge more.
